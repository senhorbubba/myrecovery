Rehab Protocol

Education only: Follow surgeon restriction and adapt based on pain levels.

Goals: Control pain and swelling, protect the ACL graft and repaired meniscus, restore full passive knee extension, begin quadriceps activation, maintain circulation, and teach safety rules for daily activities and weight-bearing.

Heel Pump (Gym Ball & Band)

4–5 × 20–30 reps daily

Prone Knee Extension (Gravity Only)

3–5 × 2 min holds

Seated Assisted Knee Extension & Flexion

3 × 8–10 reps

Wall-Supported Knee Flexion

2–3 × 6–8 reps

Quad Set (with or without EMS)

4–5 × 5 sec holds

Goals: Further reduce swelling and inflammation, improve quadriceps activation and control, safely increase knee range of motion with emphasis on extension, introduce gentle knee flexion within limits, begin hip and core activation, and improve confidence with basic daily movements.

Prone Knee Extension (Gravity + Optional Weight)

3 × 3 min

Knee Extension Exercise with EMS

3 × 8–10 reps

Clamshell (No or Light Band)

3 × 10–12 reps

Goals: Achieve and maintain full knee extension, progressively improve knee flexion range of motion, increase quadriceps strength and endurance, improve neuromuscular control of the knee, initiate general muscle strengthening (upper body, core, and non-operative leg), enhance hip stability, and normalize gait pattern within protocol restrictions.

Standing Hamstring Activation

3 × 10–12 reps

Mini Squat (0–45°)

3 × 8–10 reps

Goals: Restore functional knee flexion mobility for daily activities, significantly improve quadriceps strength and activation, strengthen hip abductors, gluteal muscles, and lateral stabilizers, improve knee stability during controlled closed-chain movements, increase tolerance to progressive lower-limb loading while respecting meniscus healing, and build confidence in controlled movement patterns.

Step-Up (Low Box, Slow Eccentric)

3 × 8–10 reps

Terminal Knee Extension (Band)

3 × 15 reps

Goals: Achieve near-normal strength of the quadriceps and surrounding musculature, improve single-leg control, balance, and proprioception, enhance coordination between hip, knee, and ankle during functional movements, increase tolerance to daily and functional loads such as stairs and prolonged walking, and prepare the knee for progression to higher-level rehabilitation activities if cleared.

Reverse Lunge

3 × 8 reps
