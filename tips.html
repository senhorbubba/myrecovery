Recovery Tips

Hard-earned lessons on managing swelling, choosing the right gear, and navigating daily life during recovery.

01 Swelling Control

Elevation - Do it Right

  • 01. Keep the knee above heart level to let gravity drain fluid.
  • 02. Use pillows under the ankle, never directly under the knee.
  • 03. 20-30 minutes, multiple times a day.

03 Traveling Strategy

The 6-Week Rule

Surgeons generally recommend waiting around 6 weeks before long-haul flights to reduce the risk of DVT (blood clots). In my case, I was on the move earlier than that: at 2 weeks post-op I did a short car trip (about 2 hours); at week 4 I took two long-haul flights back-to-back; and between weeks 4 and 8 I was traveling continuously, sightseeing, walking a lot, and, honestly, abusing my knee more than I should have. None of this was done casually: every trip was cleared by my doctor, I used prescribed blood thinners, wore compression socks, moved frequently, and monitored swelling and pain closely.

Air Travel

  • 1 Seat Choice: Book 1st row (bulkhead) or an aisle seat. You must be able to stretch the leg fully.
  • 2 Elevation: Use a carry-on or inflatable pillow under your foot during the flight.
  • 3 Wheelchair Service: Essential. Save your knee for the destination, not the airport terminal.

Car Travel

  • 1 Position: Sit in the back seat. Stretch the operated leg horizontally across the seat bench.
  • 2 Frequency: Stop every 45-60 minutes. Get out, walk, and perform 10 ankle pumps.
  • 3 Ice: Pack a portable cooler with ice packs for immediate relief upon arrival.

Sightseeing & Pacing

Support

Wear your compression brace. It provides the "structural confidence" needed for uneven surfaces or long museum halls.

Hydration

Travel causes inflammation. Drink 2x more water than usual and keep salt intake low to prevent the joint from filling with fluid.

The "Stop" Rule

If the knee starts to throb or feel "full," your day is over. Pushing through leads to 48 hours of setback.

04 Do vs Don't

The Do's

  • ✓ Do protect extension from day one, everything builds on it.
  • ✓ Do treat swelling management as a daily discipline.
  • ✓ Do move often, even on rest days (circulation matters).
  • ✓ Do respect fatigue, physical and mental.
  • ✓ Do track progress in small metrics: degrees, control, confidence.
  • ✓ Do plan recovery with the same intention as training.
  • ✓ Do stay consistent. Boring rehab done daily beats heroic effort.

The Don'ts

  • ✕ Don't put a pillow directly under the knee (it kills extension).
  • ✕ Don't chase pain-free days as proof you’re “ready”.
  • ✕ Don't ignore swelling, it’s often more important than pain.
  • ✕ Don't stack hard days (walking, travel, training) without recovery.
  • ✕ Don't rush flexion at the expense of extension.
  • ✕ Don't skip basics because they feel “too easy”.
  • ✕ Don't assume pushing harder always speeds things up, it often slows them down.
