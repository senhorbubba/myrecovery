Week 1 Nov 20, 2025

Day 4: Testing Limits

"Day 4 was about testing limits - reducing pain medication, re-entering social life, and starting real movement. The body pushed back with stiffness, fatigue, and poor sleep, reminding me that progress isn’t linear and recovery still demands discipline."

Pain Level 6/10
Swelling 8/10

Woke up at 6:00.

Today, I decided to try stopping Tramal and rely on Novalgina instead. First experiment with less pain medication. I was also planning my first social meeting outside the house. Let’s see how that goes.

In the morning, I started with foot pumps and used the roller to move my leg forward and back - exactly as my doctor recommended. It’s much harder than I expected. Stiff. Painful. Resistant. The knee doesn’t want to move yet, even though the mind does.

Later, I went to see a friend. To get there, I had to climb a lot of stairs - not elegant, not fast, but manageable. It felt good to see people. To talk. To be somewhere else.

Sitting with your leg elevated in a social setting is awkward. Still, the words, the presence, the normality helped more than I anticipated.

I did make a mistake.

I drank some wine. Not much, but combined with the medication, it didn’t go well. I felt unusually tired, slightly nauseous, and very sleepy.

I came back home and went straight to bed. Sleep wasn’t great. Was it because I stopped Tramal? The alcohol? The heavy meal? Probably a mix of all three. That’s a signal. Time to return to cleaner eating and more discipline.

Recovery keeps teaching the same lesson: Small choices matter.
