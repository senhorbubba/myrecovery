Week 1 Nov 17, 2025

Day 1: Pain, Swelling, and Learning Patience

"The first full day after surgery brought swelling, pain, medication routines, and total muscle shutdown. Between learning crutches, working through the fog, and leaning on family and friends, Day 1 was about managing discomfort and accepting how vulnerable recovery really feels."

Pain Level 10/10
Swelling 10/10

Woke up at 6:40, gently and not so gently, by my kids. They immediately gathered around the bed. Looking at my leg. Touching it. Asking questions. Curious, worried, fascinated. I was still exhausted but couldn’t fall back asleep.

Today was about starting the routine.

The leg is swollen. Heavy. Angry.

Medication schedule: • Toragesic: every 12h (2 days) • Tramal: every 6h (2 days) • Xarelto 10 mg: once a day (15 days) • Zinnat 500 mg: every 8h (7 days) • Novalgina: every 6h (7 days)

I did what everyone does now: researched what other people take, what mixes well, what doesn’t. ChatGPT and Google both seemed to know everything. Still, my conclusion was simple - if you trust your doctor, follow the plan. He’s done this hundreds of times. He knows the surgery you had and what works for your case.

Physically, it’s strange.

I can’t really move my leg. No power in my quads at all. They hurt - like the feeling after an extreme workout. Which makes no sense, because I haven’t trained my legs in over a week. Still, the soreness is real.

I learned how to use crutches properly. Especially the small but important things: lifting the leg, positioning it on pillows, elevating it without activating muscles that simply don’t respond yet.

I went back to work online. Surprisingly, it felt good. A bit surreal - and yes, working slightly “high” on painkillers is.. interesting.

Drinking a lot of water. Peeing often. Pain is still intense, even with medication. I get tired fast. I sleep in short blocks, constantly searching for a position that works. What feels okay one hour doesn’t work the next.

I read a lot - about recovery timelines, travel restrictions, what comes next.

Mentally, it’s heavy. Sad. Pissed. Impatient. Vulnerable.

Every call with a friend or family member helps. Even short conversations lift the mood. They pull you out of your own head for a moment.

Keep your close ones close. They help more than you realize. They make you forget, even if just for a bit.
