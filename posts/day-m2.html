Pre-op Nov 14, 2025

Day-2: Emergency Room

"A long day in the ER: pain, waiting, uncertainty, and small moments of relief. Seeing others reminded me to be grateful, and having family close made all the difference."

Pain Level 1/10
Swelling 10/10

12:00 - Arrival Entered the emergency room around noon. Because of my insurance, this had to go through an emergency procedure. Otherwise, approval would take 30–40 days. Not really a choice.

Waiting Room Reality The ER was full of people with problems much bigger and more serious than mine. Strangely, that helped. It made me feel grateful that it’s “only” a knee. Earlier, it felt like the end of the world.

The Long Wait Waited a long time to get a bed. Tests followed: blood work, X-ray, questionnaires. Still in a lot of pain.

17:00 - Finally a Bed Got admitted around 5 PM. Velflon in, first medications administered. Not even sure what they gave me - but it worked. Pain eased noticeably.

Uncertainty Still unclear whether surgery would happen tomorrow or on Sunday. MRI was scheduled only for 21:00, so surgery tomorrow seemed unlikely. Feeling slightly annoyed with the doctor’s assistant - not very direct, which made the uncertainty harder to deal with.

Family Visit My family came to visit. The room was a mess and that annoyed me, but their presence helped a lot. Being reminded that you’re not alone makes a real difference.

Small Tip Bring a book. It helps kill time - and you can even take it with you into the MRI room.
